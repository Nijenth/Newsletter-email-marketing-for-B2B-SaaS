# Sources: Newsletter / Email Marketing for B2B SaaS

This is my list of the 10 experts I picked for this research, why I picked them, and links to where they actually publish.

## How I picked these 10

My bar was simple: has this person actually built or run a real newsletter, email program, or email company, not just written generic tips about email marketing? I cared more about verifiable track records (companies they built, subscriber numbers, revenue results) than follower counts.

What genuinely surprised me while researching this was the newsletter business-economics angle. I went in assuming newsletters were mostly about writing well and growing a list. Matt McGarry's data changed that for me, he points out that B2B newsletter sponsorships run $100-500 CPM versus $25-40 for typical consumer newsletters. That's not a small difference. It reframed how I think about why B2B newsletters specifically are worth building, beyond just "thought leadership."

The hardest judgment call wasn't finding 10 names, it was deciding how many people to include from each angle. There's a real temptation to just grab 10 newsletter-growth people since that's the easiest category to find strong names in. I made myself spread the list across newsletter economics, SaaS lifecycle email, cold outreach, onboarding sequences, and copywriting craft, because I think a useful future playbook needs all of those, not just "how to grow a newsletter."

## A name I cut, and why

I almost included Chase Dimond, he's driven over $200M in email-attributable revenue and has 438K+ LinkedIn followers, genuinely impressive numbers. But when I actually looked closer, his entire practice is ecommerce/DTC, brands like Poo-Pourri, Eight Sleep, Mixtiles, not B2B SaaS. Including him would've been picking an impressive name over an actually-fitting one, which is exactly what this assignment said not to do. I swapped him for Florin Tatulea instead, who's fully B2B SaaS-native.

## A few notes on how I did this

* I verified every name myself through web search instead of trusting existing "top X" lists at face value. A few names from generic "email marketing influencer" roundups got cut once I actually checked whether they had B2B SaaS-specific, verifiable experience.
* I tried to spread this list across different angles on purpose, newsletter business mechanics, SaaS lifecycle email, cold outreach, onboarding sequences, copywriting craft, and data-driven research, instead of 10 people all teaching the same newsletter-growth tactics.
* I didn't scrape LinkedIn for this research, since that violates their Terms of Service. Anywhere I reference someone's LinkedIn content, it's manually read, summarized in my own words, and linked back to the original.
